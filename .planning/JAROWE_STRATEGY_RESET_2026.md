# JAROWE.COM STRATEGY RESET
## What jarowe.com should actually become next

March 20, 2026

---

## Executive call

The strongest version of `jarowe.com` is not "everything possible on the internet."

That framing is too broad. It turns a distinctive world into a miscellaneous portal.

The strongest version is:

**jarowe.com = the most alive personal world on the internet**

It should feel like:

- a place with weather, time, mood, memory, and surprise
- a creative host who can guide and act
- a living personal universe that changes daily
- a launchpad into creation, not just a gallery of finished work

The site should make people feel three things:

1. "I have never seen a personal site feel like this."
2. "There is something new for me today."
3. "I want to make something now."

That is the category.

Not portfolio.
Not blog.
Not app launcher.
Not "AI tools plus news plus random experiments."

It is a **living world for wonder and activation**.

---

## The hard truth

The current risk is not lack of ambition. It is ambition without hierarchy.

If `jarowe.com` tries to become:

- a personal site
- a positive news network
- a real-time 3D lab
- a creator operating system
- a social community
- a playable metaverse
- a generic AI concierge

all at once, it will lose the one thing that makes it memorable: **a specific human point of view**.

The strategic move is not to do less interesting work.
It is to give every idea the right orbit.

### Correct orbit model

**Core world: `jarowe.com`**
- Wonder
- Presence
- Daily return
- Personal mythology

**Creation layer: Starseed Labs**
- Canvas
- notes
- idea incubation
- project kickoffs
- creative utilities

**Guide layer: Glint**
- navigation
- memory
- suggestions
- tool use
- activation

**Signal layer: positive/progress/news**
- a mode, section, or channel
- not the homepage identity

---

## Product thesis

`jarowe.com` should be built around this promise:

**"Come here to feel more alive, leave here with a spark."**

That means the site needs five product jobs:

### 1. Arrival

Deliver a first-15-seconds "this is different" moment.

Current leverage:
- cinematic globe
- music
- Glint
- bento density
- constellation/world metaphor

### 2. Orientation

Help a new visitor understand what this place is without flattening the magic.

The site needs a clearer top-level mental model:

- **Earth**: real-time world, travel, weather, signal
- **Constellation**: life, work, ideas, memory
- **Play**: games, puzzles, secrets, experiments
- **Create**: Starseed canvas, notes, prompts, project launch
- **Glint**: the guide who can move between all of them

### 3. Return

Give people a concrete reason to come back tomorrow.

Not just "there might be something new."
There must be a visible daily engine.

### 4. Activation

Turn passive browsing into action.

The site should not only display Jared's creativity.
It should provoke visitors into making, saving, sketching, collecting, or sharing something.

### 5. Progression

Reward deeper engagement.

The existing XP/streak/secret structure is an asset. It should be tied to discovery, creation, and return, not just novelty.

## What belongs on jarowe.com

### Core experiences to strengthen

#### Living Earth
- Real weather
- air quality
- daylight/night state
- moon phase
- region mood
- selected world signals

This makes the site feel physically situated in time.

#### Constellation
- the deepest expression of the brand
- the proof that this is not a normal portfolio
- the place where personal history becomes explorable structure

This remains the long-term moat.

#### Glint
- from mascot to operator
- from chat toy to site orchestrator

Glint should be the fastest way to:
- understand the site
- move through it
- surface hidden things
- start creating

#### Daily ritual
- one daily thing to discover
- one daily thing to do
- one daily thing to carry away

Example stack:
- daily portal
- daily prompt
- daily progress signal
- daily secret or unlock

#### Starseed entrypoint
- simple scratchpad
- idea capture
- "make something from this"

This is how jarowe.com graduates from admiration to participation.

## What does not belong on jarowe.com as the core identity

### 1. A full generic news site

Bad fit as the primary brand.

Reason:
- it shifts attention away from the world itself
- it creates editorial overhead
- it risks turning wonder into feed consumption

Better move:
- build a **progress signal** layer
- keep it curated, visual, selective, and world-shaped

### 2. "Everything app" positioning

Bad fit because it sounds expansive but feels vague.

Visitors need a strong noun.
The noun is not "everything."
The noun is **world** or **living place**.

### 3. Full multiplayer as a near-term priority

Interesting, but not core right now.

Until the single-player loop is magnetic, multiplayer mostly adds noise, moderation burden, and engineering drag.

### 4. Splat-first navigation

Gaussian splats are compelling, but they should be premium scenes, not the default shell.

They are best used as:
- memory capsules
- hidden portals
- featured place-based moments
- special exhibitions

Not as the main information architecture.

## Strategic recommendation by surface

## A. Home page

The home page should become a **daily stateful foyer**.

It should answer:

- What is alive here today?
- Where should I go first?
- What can I make from here?

### Add now

- "Today on jarowe.com" hero state
- one rotating daily invitation from Glint
- one live world condition card
- one progress signal card
- one creation prompt card
- one featured portal

Success metric:
- visitor sees something date-specific in under 5 seconds

---

## B. Glint

Glint should become the operating layer, not just the conversational layer.

### Core role

Glint is the host who can:
- explain
- navigate
- reveal
- create a starting point
- hand off into Starseed

### Recommended bounded tool set

Phase 1 tool actions:
- `open_page(route)`
- `focus_constellation_node(id)`
- `start_globe_tour(region)`
- `show_today_signal()`
- `create_starseed_canvas(template)`
- `create_note(title, seed)`
- `save_idea(text)`

Phase 2 tool actions:
- `start_daily_prompt_session()`
- `subscribe_digest(email)`
- `queue_topic(topic)`
- `generate_share_card(kind)`

Phase 3 tool actions:
- authenticated account/setup flows with explicit confirmation

### Important constraint

Glint should never feel like an unbounded general-purpose chatbot.

He should feel like a **site-native intelligence with agency inside this world**.

That is much more memorable.

---

## C. Starseed Labs

Starseed should absorb the broad "creator utility" ambition.

That means:
- idea boards
- scratch notes
- visual planning
- launch templates
- AI kickoffs

The best mental model:

**jarowe.com inspires. Starseed helps you begin.**

This is the clean split.

### Best first version

- Excalidraw-powered infinite sketch canvas
- Milkdown-powered notes/doc surface
- Glint handoff:
  - "Start a board from this idea"
  - "Turn this article into a brainstorm"
  - "Make me a project starter"

This makes the world generative instead of purely observational.

---

## D. Positive news / progress layer

This should exist, but not as a generic feed.

The right move is a **progress lens**:

- small number of meaningful signals
- highly visual
- geographically grounded when possible
- linked to action or curiosity

Examples:
- "What got better today"
- "A progress signal from somewhere on Earth"
- "A chart that changes how you see the world"
- "One quiet breakthrough"

### Best format

Use three source types:

1. **Data sources**
- Our World in Data
- official public datasets
- climate / health / development charts

2. **News metadata**
- headline feeds and summaries
- link out to original reporting

3. **Curated constructive journalism**
- Good Good Good
- Fix The News
- The Progress Network

### Product note

The progress layer should produce hope through clarity, not through empty positivity.

The tone should be:
- intelligent
- measured
- constructive
- not saccharine

---

## E. Gaussian splats and immersive 3D

This is real and worth doing, but selectively.

### What the research suggests

- Gaussian splatting is now production-credible for web viewing and editing
- PlayCanvas has a full workflow around capture, viewing, editing, and publishing
- PlayCanvas also exposes procedural splats, but that path is still beta
- splat rendering remains performance-sensitive because fill rate and overdraw are expensive

### Product recommendation

Use splats for **memory theaters**, not for global app structure.

Best use cases for jarowe.com:
- enter a real scanned location from Jared's life
- attach a story and soundtrack to the scene
- hide one inside the constellation as a rare unlock
- use one on a campaign or release page

Best use cases for Starseed / experiment layer:
- procedural splat toys
- paintable splat surfaces
- image-to-splat and text-to-splat experiments

### Not recommended yet

- splat-heavy homepage shell
- multiple giant splat scenes loaded by default
- betting the whole navigation model on splats

## Growth model

If the goal is attention, activation, and habitual use, the growth loop should be:

### 1. Intrigue

People arrive because the site is visually distinct and socially shareable.

Acquisition vectors:
- short clips of the living globe / constellation / splat portals
- daily share cards
- "Glint made this for me" artifacts
- release and music campaigns feeding back into the world

### 2. Ritual

They return because there is a daily pattern.

Needed daily loop:
- a visible daily state
- one new path
- one fresh prompt
- one live signal

### 3. Agency

They engage because they can do something meaningful.

Agency actions:
- sketch
- save an idea
- get a Glint plan
- collect something
- unlock something

### 4. Identity

They stay because the site feels like a worldview, not a content bucket.

### 5. Contribution

Only after the above:
- guestbook
- collaborative notes
- community constellation fragments
- light presence

## The best near-term concept

If the entire site needs one sentence:

**jarowe.com is a living world of wonder that turns curiosity into creation.**

If it needs one stronger product shape:

**A personal world with a daily pulse, an AI guide, and portals into memory, play, and making.**

## The 90-day plan

## Phase 1: clarify the promise

Ship in 1-2 weeks.

- tighten homepage messaging around world + daily pulse + creation
- add a visible "today" state
- add one progress signal card
- add one Glint-first CTA
- add one "start in Starseed" CTA

Outcome:
- users understand what the site is in one visit

## Phase 2: make Glint actually useful

Ship in 2-4 weeks.

- migrate Glint toward tool use
- let Glint navigate the site and start simple creation flows
- add command palette behavior through Glint and keyboard
- preserve the character voice, but prioritize action

Outcome:
- Glint becomes a memorable differentiator

## Phase 3: launch the first creation surface

Ship in 3-5 weeks.

- Starseed scratchpad
- Excalidraw canvas embed
- Milkdown notes surface
- Glint handoff into both

Outcome:
- jarowe.com stops being purely observational

## Phase 4: launch the daily engine

Ship in 2-3 weeks.

- daily prompt
- daily progress signal
- daily portal or featured path
- streaks tied to discovery + creation

Outcome:
- a real return loop exists

## Phase 5: add one premium immersive portal

Ship in 2-4 weeks.

- one curated splat memory capsule
- soundtrack + narrative + shareable entry point

Outcome:
- the site gets a new flagship wow surface without becoming structurally dependent on splats

## Recommended stack decisions

### Adopt now

- View Transition API for cross-page continuity
- Open-Meteo for weather and air quality atmosphere
- OpenAI Responses API plus function calling for Glint actionability
- Excalidraw for infinite canvas
- Milkdown for notes

### Adopt later

- OpenAI Realtime API for voice Glint after tool use is solid
- PartyKit for lightweight presence or collaboration after single-player retention exists
- Gaussian procedural splats as experimental content, not core nav

### Defer

- full multiplayer
- VR-first flows
- generalized autonomous agent behavior
- full editorial news operation

## Metrics that matter

Do not measure success only by page views.

Track:

- return rate within 7 days
- percent of visitors who trigger Glint
- percent of visitors who enter Constellation
- percent of visitors who create something in Starseed
- percent of visitors who interact with the daily layer
- share rate on generated artifacts or share cards
- median time to first meaningful action

Primary product KPI:

**percentage of visitors who do at least one intentional action**

Examples:
- ask Glint something
- open a portal
- solve a daily item
- save an idea
- create a board

---

## Final recommendation

Do not build "more internet."

Build a world.

Make it:
- alive every day
- personal without being closed
- intelligent without being generic
- beautiful without becoming vague
- useful because it activates people, not because it imitates tools they already have

The biggest unlock is this:

**jarowe.com should not be a destination because it has many features.**

**It should be a destination because it has a living point of view.**

Everything else should serve that.
